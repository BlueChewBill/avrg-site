AVRG — three directions

Same real content (your data.js + photos), three different websites. Open each, judge on phone width too.

A — The Ledger Warm paper, ink rules, serif headlines, boards catalogued like museum specimens with reference numbers. Quiet, archival, lets the wood colors carry everything. B — Night Gallery Near-black, the deadpan taglines become the giant hero. Boards hang in horizontal shelves with placards, photos melting into the dark like the marble they were shot on. C — Dye Lot Bone + ink poster energy: chunky uppercase type, hard offset shadows, tagline ticker tape, collection color highlights. Loud but tidy — skate-brand, not startup. D — Dye Lot, Shelved C's look with B's layout: every collection is a horizontal shelf of cards that bleeds off the page edge, so all decks live on one scannable page. E — The Conveyor Warm charcoal + grain + huge bone wordmark + terracotta accents. Everything centered; each collection is a slow auto-rotating carousel — center two cards big and clear, the rest dim away. Hover a side to steer, drag on touch. F — Dye Lot Conveyor (current pick) The conveyor on bone with E-style cards, hardened up. Click a deck to open an inline inspector (all angles + DM link) with the full D-style shelf revealed beneath it; click a section title to toggle the shelf on its own. G — Workshop Split F's dark look and conveyors, restructured to tell the story: process column (mold, plies, clamps) | riding video + the sell-off pitch | three shelf bands (Original / Shaped / Routed). On phones the shelves become a 2×2 grid — video plus one beauty shot per collection. H — Blackout G stripped down on pure black: no process column, no subtitle, one-line pitch. Band titles are plain white bold, and shelf cards dissolve to nothing at the edges instead of hitting a clip line.
